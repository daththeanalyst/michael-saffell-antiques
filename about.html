Our Story

Nearly 50 years of passion for British antiques

Since 1975

A Lifetime in Antiques

Jo and Michael Saffell have been passionate about antiques since 1975 — nearly fifty years of discovering, collecting and dealing in beautiful objects from Britain's past.

When they relocated to Bath, they found the perfect home: a period building on Walcot Buildings with a ready-made shop below, which they've filled with their remarkable and ever-growing collection.

Our Specialism

The World of British Tins

After moving to Bath, Jo and Michael began to specialise in what has become their great passion: antique British tins. From Victorian biscuit tins by Huntley & Palmers and Crawford's, to beautifully lithographed tobacco tins and advertising pieces — each item tells a story of British craftsmanship and commercial art.

Their collection spans over a century of British manufacturing and design, from the 1860s through to the 1960s. They deal in everything from museum-quality rarities to affordable pieces for new collectors.

Why Collect Tins

The Appeal of Antique Tins

🎨

Beautiful Design

Victorian and Edwardian tins are miniature works of art — elaborate lithography, embossing and colour that rival fine prints.

📚

Social History

Each tin is a snapshot of its era — the brands, the advertising, the tastes and the aesthetics of a bygone age.

📈

Collectible Value

Antique tins are highly collectible worldwide. Rare examples have sold for thousands at auction — and interest continues to grow.

🏠

Decorative Appeal

Beyond collecting, antique tins make wonderful decorative pieces — adding colour, character and conversation to any room.
