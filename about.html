Our Story

Nearly 50 years of passion for British antiques

Since 1975

A Lifetime in Antiques

Jo and Michael Saffell have been in the antiques trade since 1975 — nearly fifty years of discovering, collecting and dealing in beautiful objects from Britain's past. Michael was a student in Bath before becoming a dealer, and the couple initially sold stock at The Great Western Antiques Centre before going independent.

When they decided to relocate permanently to Bath, they found the perfect home: a period building on Walcot Buildings with a ready-made shop below, which they've filled with their remarkable and ever-growing collection of hard-to-come-by British tins and advertising items.

Our Specialism

The World of British Tins

After settling in Bath, Jo and Michael began to specialise in what has become their great passion: antique British tins. Their living room displays a prized collection of Huntley & Palmers oriental-style biscuit tins dating from the 1890s to 1908 — from the famous "Literature" book-shaped tin of 1901 to the "Grandfather Clock" with its chinoiserie case and moving hands (1929).

They also deal in Crawford's tartan shortbreads, Peek Freans ornate Victorians, Player's Navy Cut tobacco tins, Gold Flake cigarette cases by W.D. & H.O. Wills, enamel advertising signs for Bovril, OXO and Colman's Mustard, and confectionery tins from Cadbury's, Rowntree's and Fry's. Condition is paramount — "tin worm" (thin strands of rust travelling under the printed surface) can render otherwise fine examples valueless.

Why Collect Tins

The Appeal of Antique Tins

◆

Beautiful Design

Victorian and Edwardian tins are miniature works of art — elaborate lithography, embossing and colour that rival fine prints.

✦

Social History

Each tin is a snapshot of its era — the brands, the advertising, the tastes and the aesthetics of a bygone age.

★

Collectible Value

Antique tins are highly collectible worldwide. Rare examples have sold for thousands at auction — and interest continues to grow.

◎

Decorative Appeal

Beyond collecting, antique tins make wonderful decorative pieces — adding colour, character and conversation to any room.
